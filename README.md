# Feelings Grew Hard Under the Rule of Crumbs

A fictional account of cultural transformation in underground sentiment during the rule of American President Crumbs. Generated by Claude Sonnet 4.6.
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Crumbs administration was theatrical in ways its predecessors were not willing to be. The more liberal administrations before it had not spoken of barbarism so cleanly — they had distributed their vitriol as policy language, their fearmongering as measured concern. Crumbs divided things. People, economics, what was owed and to whom. The war in Arabia had given the preceding administration plenty of subjects on which to inflict pain, and pain was not inflicted only on Arabians, but the accounting was murkier then. Under Crumbs the lines were drawn in public.

---

Crumbs was a betting man. It has already been stated; see [crumbs_the_president_of_the_betting_man](https://github.com/Changissnz/crumbs_the_president_of_the_betting_man__claude_generated) for more information. The free market, in the second half of his rule, was in a free fall for virtually the entire period. The administration let market dynamics talk, and what the market said fed a darkness in many American citizens. Someone on NASDAQ is selling someone else's stuff at this price. This other person is worth this many billions in private equity. Most people cannot afford a mortgage. There might be another Great War on the horizon. The discrepancies were plain and they were ignored in the name of the gambler's grandeur. The darkness was a wager of death, or great loss, practically speaking. A person in the know who calls a bet is a rational player. A person not in the know believes the bets imperil their lives. There were more people not in the know.

The idea of another Great War was not as farfetched as it should have been. The socioeconomics and lawfare throughout the country had brought that form of desperation into open consideration. The old 1950s Cold War nuclear geometries had shrunk considerably. The Schlavian Civil War, as mentioned in works such as [brittania_wanted_to_matter](https://github.com/Changissnz/brittania_wanted_to_matter__claude_generated), [brittania-s_bots_at_your_service](https://github.com/Changissnz/brittania-s_bots_at_your_service__claude_generated), and [unsolvable_trends_in_the_west](https://github.com/Changissnz/unsolvable_trends_in_the_west__claude_generated), played hand in hand with the reawakening of the Germanian and Hispanian kernels in Western Europe, both seeking to autonomize themselves during the period of American international retreat.

---

Most people in the developed world were not always laughing and having good times, sharing intimate moments, having sex during the rule of Crumbs. Those privileges were reserved for the populist heads of Crumbs' society: girls from prominent families, strong and muscular athletes from Rome, barons from the Schinian mainland and vassal states with their divvying up of stock profits. Those were the kinds of people having a great and fantastic time during the second half of the Crumbs administration.

---

Brittania had succumbed to the many idiocrasies shared by Brittanian and Arabian royals. One failed tactic after the next. Instead of pursuing the deals Hispania was solidifying with organizations such as BRICS, Brittania thought itself better than Hispania, despite Hispania's meteoric rise, demonstrated in works such as [a_little_story_on_hispania-s_meteoric_rise](https://github.com/Changissnz/a_little_story_on_hispania-s_meteoric_rise__claude_generated). It set out to rival Germania. This did not work out well for Brittania's subjects. The Brittanian kernel was not rich in resources. Mughalia and India were growing less fond of Brittania by the day. The American-Schinian trade wars started by Crumbs gave Mughalia and India more bargaining power. Dedollarization campaigns by BRICS — an organization that faltered frequently but could still expend just as much more — made bargains more common. Arabia had been growing its influence in Brittanian politics and was sympathetic to Germania's causes. Those sympathies derailed most of Brittania's efforts.

---

Feelings themselves rarely made a difference besides from the moments. In years long ago it was not just feelings that made man and woman husband and wife. In those years, that principle still held. The West was not on a full retreat. But the world was shrinking. Bridges collapsed. Communication got shuttered. Roles became more fixed.
